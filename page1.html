The Story Behind Acharya Ayurveda

A Vision of a Visionary- Acharya Adinadhan

"Health is a right and not business. A hospital is not a profit centre, but a place where every individual from every section of society gets the same quality affordable treatment!" - Acharya Adinadhan

This vision of my dad, having come from a traditional Ayurveda family, made him start his first charitable Ayurvedic clinic in the name of 'Uthradom Vaidyasala' in Trivandrum, Kerala for helping and improving the life of people who could not afford quality healthcare.

Until then Ayurveda, though effective, was expensive, which only the rich people could afford. To facilitate the treatments affordably and to get the best results, my father decided to manufacture medicines in-house. The medicines were so effective, the demand was more and the supply less. Gradually, with no advertising or marketing, the small clinic grew into a full-fledged day clinic with more facilities and pharmacy where people would pour-in from different parts of Kerala for treatments.

The only reason for our success is my dad's vision of serving the community with a genuine intention to bring light into the lives of people who could not afford it. Coming from a family of traditional vaidyas, he lived the life of a Yogi, helping the underprivilaged people get medicines and treatments without spending a dime!

When my dad passed away, I was moved to see hundreds of thousands of people grieving, visiting my house everyday, telling us stories of how my dad had helped them in a big way. People used to call him "Acharya" with love, respect and affection and he was a true leader in their minds.

I, as his daughter, have embarked on this responsible journey of continuing the charitable intiatives that my dad started in providing health and happiness for all, even after he has gone- only with the intention of making him proud everyday wherever he is now!

We miss you dad!

Dr Rekha Jithin BAMS
Daughter, Acharya Adinadhan
